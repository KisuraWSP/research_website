Description

Our research project is a multi-disciplinary system that integrates virtual reality, artificial intelligence, behavioral simulation, VR timeline and Extincition Event Simulation into a single ecosystem. Its centerpiece is an immersive Prehistoric VR Museum designed for the Meta Quest 3 headset, supported by AI-driven interactions, dynamic animal behavior, and interactive educational features.

Project Scope

Literature Survey

Traditional prehistoric education—textbooks, documentaries, and static exhibits—is often passive, text-heavy, and low in engagement, limiting memory retention and attention. Existing VR projects, including 360° videos and animated reconstructions, provide more immersion but remain mostly static and scripted. Advances in VR and AI offer interactive, immersive learning opportunities. VR allows users to explore inaccessible environments and visualize complex concepts, while AI enables spatially aware guides that provide context-sensitive explanations and personalized interactions. Studies in STEM and museums show immersive VR improves comprehension, retention, and spatial reasoning.

Previous AI behavior modeling using rule-based systems or fuzzy logic lacks adaptability and realism. Integrating machine learning with VR allows autonomous prehistoric animals to respond to environmental conditions, social dynamics, and internal states. Projects like Dinosaurs and Crvena Stijena VR demonstrate interest in immersive prehistoric experiences but still rely on static storytelling and limited interactivity. This project addresses these gaps by creating an AI-driven, spatially aware VR environment with dynamic animal behaviors, interactive activities, and memory- and attention-enhancing techniques, transforming passive exploration into active, engaging learning.

Research Gap

AI-Driven Prehistoric Animal Interactions in VR

Existing VR exhibits rarely include scalable and believable AI animals. We propose herd/predator logic with scene-aware behaviors.

Enhancing Memory & Attention in VR Learning

We add timeline quests, spatial anchors, and spaced-recall cues to improve focus and learning outcomes.

Beyond Static Storytelling in Prehistoric VR

From passive viewing to dynamic scenes: reactive narration and hands-on interactions.

Context-Aware AI Guides

Guides read the room: surfaces, exhibit proximity, and player location for natural interactions.

Research Problem & Solution

Research Problem

Describe the central challenge (e.g., low engagement/retention with static VR exhibits; limited interactivity and adaptive guidance).

Proposed Solution

An AI-guided, scene-aware VR museum featuring dynamic animal simulations, a playable timeline, and memory enhancers.

Research Objectives

  • Design a context-aware VR guide with spatial triggers.
  • Implement animal behavior (herding, predator-prey) with ML-Agents.
  • Build a playable prehistoric timeline with narration.
  • Evaluate learning/retention via user studies.

Methodology

1. Prototype & Scene Setup

Environment blockout, interaction patterns, and baseline navigation (teleport + direct locomotion).

2. AI & Simulation

Herding, predator detection, feeding interactions, and narrator triggers linked to exhibits.

3. Evaluation

Usability testing, knowledge retention measures, and iteration.
